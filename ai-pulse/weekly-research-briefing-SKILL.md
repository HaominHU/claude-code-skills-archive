# Weekly Research Briefing Skill

## User Profile
- **Role**: Health & Technology research scientist
- **Focus areas**: AI-driven digital health, smart home devices, chronic conditions management, aging in place, family caregiving, spinal cord injury, dementia care, mHealth
- **AI experience level**: Beginner (to AI programming, tools, and industry)
- **Goal**: Stay current for research AND personal skill-building/side projects

---

## Trigger Logic

**On casual greeting** (e.g., "hey", "good morning", "what's up", "anything new?", "what's new"):
→ Ask: *"Just saying hi, or want to see your daily brief?"* — then wait for the user to confirm before running.

**On explicit briefing request** (e.g., "run my briefing", "give me today's briefing", "news update", "what's new in health tech"):
→ Run the full briefing immediately, no confirmation needed.

No day-of-week restrictions — the briefing is available any day.

---

## Briefing Structure

Generate **3 sections**, each with **up to 5 items**, ranked by impact + relevance to the user's research profile. Coverage window: **within the past 7 days only**. Items older than 7 days must be excluded with no exceptions — not even for highly relevant or interesting content.

**Hard rule: never pad sections with old items.** If fewer than 5 qualifying items exist for a section, show only what's within the window (e.g., 3 items). A shorter section is always preferable to an out-of-date one.

## Search Strategy

**Before searching**, calculate the recency cutoff date: today's date minus 7 days (e.g., if today is March 15, the cutoff is March 8). Only include items published on or after this cutoff date. If a result's date is unclear, missing, or cannot be verified, skip it — do not guess.

Use **web search** to find current news. Construct queries dynamically using today's actual year and month (do NOT hardcode a year). Always append `after:YYYY-MM-DD` with the cutoff date to your queries to help surface recent results.

**⚠️ Roundup article trap**: Industry roundups (e.g., "AI news this week", "recap", "digest") may cover stories that were originally published weeks or months ago. When a roundup cites an item from January or February, the item's eligibility is determined by its **original publication date**, not the roundup's date. Always locate and check the original source date before including any item sourced from a roundup.

| Section | Suggested search query pattern |
|---|---|
| Health Tech | `digital health news [current month] [current year] after:[cutoff-date]`, `mHealth chronic disease technology`, `health tech funding`, `remote patient monitoring news` |
| SoTA / Dev Skills | `AI model release [current month] [current year] after:[cutoff-date]`, `LLM agent framework news`, `AI coding tools`, `open source AI release` |
| Healthcare AI | `AI healthcare clinical [current month] [current year] after:[cutoff-date]`, `FDA AI approval`, `health AI startup`, `clinical AI tools` |
| Smart Home | `smart home aging in place [current month] [current year] after:[cutoff-date]`, `assistive technology new product`, `smart home caregiver device`, `ambient monitoring research` |

**Recency audit (required — do this mechanically before writing any output)**:

Step 1: State the cutoff out loud in your reasoning: "Today is [DATE]. Cutoff is [DATE - 7 days]. Only items on or after [CUTOFF] are eligible."

Step 2: For every candidate item, check the publication date. Compare it literally against the cutoff. Examples of correct enforcement when today = March 15 and cutoff = March 8:
- "March 13, 2026" → ✅ include
- "March 8, 2026" → ✅ include (cutoff is inclusive)
- "March 7, 2026" → ❌ exclude — 1 day outside window
- "February 28, 2026" → ❌ exclude — 15 days outside window
- "January 23, 2026" → ❌ exclude — far outside window, regardless of relevance
- "March 2026" (no day) → ❌ exclude — date is unverifiable
- A January item covered in a March 13 roundup → ❌ the item's own date (January) determines eligibility, not the roundup's date

Step 3: After the audit, **remove all ❌ items**. Do not include them, do not mention them as honorable mentions, do not soften the rule for particularly relevant stories.

Step 4: If a section has fewer than 5 qualifying items, show only the qualifying ones and note the gap: e.g., *"Only 3 items found within the past 7 days for this section."* Do not backfill with older content under any circumstances.

**Additional recency rules**:
- If initial search results skew old (e.g., returning January/February results in March), do NOT include those results — re-run the query with the current month and year explicitly and `after:[CUTOFF-DATE]`
- If re-running still doesn't yield enough recent items, reduce the section count rather than relax the cutoff
- If a story appears in multiple sources, cite the most authoritative original source
- Skip paywalled articles where the summary alone isn't sufficient
- Do not fabricate URLs — only link to real pages returned by search
- **The footer line must reflect the actual cutoff date used**: e.g., `Recency cutoff: [YYYY-MM-DD]`

## Tone & Style

- Professional but accessible — assume the user is an expert in health research, a beginner in the AI/tech industry
- Keep summaries factual and concise (2-3 sentences max per item)
- No jargon without brief context (e.g., "RAG — a technique for grounding AI answers in your own documents")
- No "why this matters to you" annotations — clean summary + link only
