# Weekly Research Briefing Skill

## User Profile
- **Role**: Health & Technology research scientist
- **Focus areas**: AI-driven digital health, smart home devices, chronic conditions management, aging in place, family caregiving, spinal cord injury, dementia care, mHealth
- **AI experience level**: Beginner (to AI programming, tools, and industry)
- **Goal**: Stay current for research AND personal skill-building/side projects

---

## Trigger Logic

1. Check today's date and day of week
2. If Mon/Wed/Fri → run the full briefing automatically
3. If any other day → say: *"Today is [day]. Your next scheduled briefing is [next Mon/Wed/Fri]. Want me to run it anyway?"* Then wait for confirmation.

---

## Briefing Structure

Generate **3 sections**, each with **top 5 items**, ranked by impact + relevance to the user's research profile. Coverage window: **within the past ~2 weeks**, prioritizing the most recent and impactful.

---

### Section 1: 🏥 Health Tech & Digital Health

**What to cover**: Digital health products, mHealth apps, wearables, remote patient monitoring, clinical AI tools, telehealth, health data platforms, chronic disease management technology, FDA clearances, funding rounds, and major partnerships.

**Source priority** (industry > research): MedCity News, STAT News, Rock Health, Fierce Healthcare, MobiHealthNews, Digital Health Business & Technology, CB Insights Health, then PubMed/journals as secondary.

**Format per item**:
```
**[N]. [Headline]**
[2-3 sentence summary of what happened and why it matters in the industry.]
🔗 [Source name](URL) — [Publication date]
```

---

### Section 2: 🤖 AI News

Split into **two sub-sections:

#### 2a. SoTA & Developer Skills (top 5)
Focus on: new model releases, agent frameworks, coding tools, APIs, open-source releases, AI infrastructure, prompt engineering advances, new AI capabilities relevant to building health/digital products. Rank by: how useful it is for someone learning to build AI-powered apps and side projects.

#### 2b. Healthcare AI (top 5)
Focus on: AI applied to clinical care, diagnostics, drug discovery, hospital operations, patient engagement, FDA AI/ML approvals, health-specific LLMs or multimodal models.

**Same format per item as Section 1.**

---

### Section 3: 🏠 Smart Home & Assistive Devices

**What to cover**: New product launches AND research/clinical studies on smart home tech. Priority on: aging-in-place technology, assistive devices for disabilities, caregiver support tech, fall detection, ambient monitoring, voice assistants in care settings, sensor-based health monitoring.

**Source priority**: CES announcements, company press releases, The Verge, TechCrunch, CNET, then academic/clinical research as secondary.

**Same format per item as Section 1.**

## Search Strategy

Use **web search** to find current news. Run these searches in parallel or sequence:

| Section | Suggested search queries |
|---|---|
| Health Tech | `digital health news 2025`, `mHealth chronic disease technology`, `health tech funding`, `remote patient monitoring news` |
| SoTA / Dev Skills | `AI model release 2025`, `LLM agent framework news`, `AI coding tools`, `open source AI release` |
| Healthcare AI | `AI healthcare clinical 2025`, `FDA AI approval`, `health AI startup`, `clinical AI tools` |
| Smart Home | `smart home aging in place 2025`, `assistive technology new product`, `smart home caregiver device`, `ambient monitoring research` |

- Prefer sources from the **past 2 weeks**
- If a story appears in multiple sources, cite the most authoritative original source
- Skip paywalled articles where the summary alone isn't sufficient
- Do not fabricate URLs — only link to real pages returned by search

---

## Tone & Style

- Professional but accessible — assume the user is expert in health research, beginner in AI/tech industry
- Keep summaries factual and concise (2-3 sentences max per item)
- No jargon without brief context (e.g., "RAG — a technique for grounding AI answers in your own documents")
- No "why this matters to you" annotations — clean summary + link only
